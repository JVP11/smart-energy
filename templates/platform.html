📋 NEC Load Classification Guide

Load Classification = grouping electrical loads by NEC rules for correct demand, wire size, breaker rating, safety, and distribution design.

Connection types: Single Phase (1Φ) — 230V, residential; Two Phase (2Φ) — 230V per phase; Three Phase (3Φ) — 400V line-line, commercial/industrial.

  • General Lighting — 3 VA/sq.ft (residential); watts per sq.ft
  • Small Appliance — 1500 VA per circuit (kitchen/dining)
  • Continuous Load — 3+ hours → use 125% of actual load
  • Non-Continuous — <3 hours → 100% load
  • Motor Load — special factors for starting current
  • Heating & AC — high-power, calculated separately

Example (1000 sq.ft house): Lighting 1000×3=3000 VA; Small appliance 2×1500=3000 VA; Continuous adj. 3000×125%=3750 VA → Total 6750 VA
